LLMs, SLMs & RLHF
The current frontier — massive language models that emergently acquire reasoning, coding, and instruction-following capabilities at scale. Understanding why they work (and why they fail) is increasingly essential.
Architecture of a Modern LLM
  • Decoder-only Transformer (GPT-style)
  • Causal (masked) self-attention — each token attends to previous tokens only
  • RoPE positional encodings (extends to long contexts better)
  • GroupedQuery Attention (GQA): fewer KV heads than Q heads → faster inference
  • RMSNorm (not LayerNorm) — faster
  • SwiGLU activation in FFN layers
  • Flash Attention for memory-efficient attention
Scaling Laws
$$\begin{aligned} Loss \propto (C/Copt)^{-\alpha C} \quad \text{[compute scaling]} \\ Chinchilla: N_params \approx 20 \times N_tokens (optimal) \end{aligned}$$
  • Hoffman et al. (Chinchilla): for a given compute budget, train a smaller model on more tokens, not a larger model on fewer
  • Emergent abilities: capabilities appear suddenly at certain scale thresholds
  • Power law relationship between model size, data, compute, and performance
RLHF — Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback
$$\begin{aligned} \text{Stage 1: Pre-train LLM on massive text corpus} \\ \text{Stage 2: Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT) on high-quality demonstrations} \\ \text{Stage 3: Train Reward Model (RM) on human preference pairs (A vs B)} \\ \text{Stage 4: PPO: } \max\, \mathbb{E}[\text{RM(response)}] - \beta\cdot\text{KL}(\pi \| \pi_{\text{SFT}}) \end{aligned}$$
The KL penalty prevents the model from drifting too far from the SFT base — without it, the model would learn to "hack" the reward model with degenerate outputs. RLHF is what makes ChatGPT/Claude helpful, harmless, and honest — not just a next-token predictor.

🎯 Interview Gold

"The LLM training pipeline: pre-train on internet-scale data (self-supervised, next-token prediction) → SFT on demonstration data → RLHF to align with human preferences. RLHF is what transforms a weird text-completer into a helpful assistant. For production deployment: 4-bit quantization + vLLM/TGI for batched serving + RAG for knowledge freshness. Hallucination is the core challenge — mitigated by grounding via RAG, careful prompt engineering, and confidence calibration."
