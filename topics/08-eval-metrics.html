Evaluation Metrics
Choosing the wrong metric is one of the most common mistakes in applied ML. The metric must reflect business reality, not just mathematical convenience.
Regression Metrics
$$\begin{aligned} \text{MAE} &= \frac{1}{n}\sum|y_i - \hat{y}_i| \\ \text{MSE} &= \frac{1}{n}\sum(y_i - \hat{y}_i)^2 \\ \text{RMSE} &= \sqrt{\text{MSE}} \\ R^2 &= 1 - \frac{SS_\text{res}}{SS_\text{tot}} \quad [0 = \text{mean model},\; 1 = \text{perfect}] \\ \text{MAPE} &= \frac{1}{n}\sum\frac{|y_i - \hat{y}_i|}{|y_i|} \times 100\% \end{aligned}$$
  • MAE: interpretable, robust to outliers, same units as y
  • RMSE: penalizes large errors heavily; differentiable; outlier sensitive
  • R²: proportion of variance explained; use adjusted R² for multiple features
  • MAPE: percentage error; unstable when y ≈ 0
Classification Metrics
$$\begin{aligned} \text{Accuracy} &= (TP+TN)/(TP+TN+FP+FN) \\ \text{Precision} &= TP/(TP+FP) \quad \text{(quality of positives)} \\ \text{Recall} &= TP/(TP+FN) \quad \text{(coverage of positives)} \\ F_1 &= 2 \cdot P \cdot R / (P+R) \quad \text{(harmonic mean)} \\ F_\beta &= (1+\beta^2) \cdot P \cdot R \;/\; (\beta^2 \cdot P + R) \end{aligned}$$
  • Accuracy: misleading when classes imbalanced (99% neg → always predict neg = 99% accuracy)
  • Precision: cost of false alarms (spam filter)
  • Recall: cost of missing positives (cancer detection)
  • F1: balance of both; F2 weights recall more; F0.5 weights precision more
Metric Selection Framework
Use CasePrimary MetricWhy
Fraud detection (imbalanced)PR-AUC, Recall@FPRMissing fraud is costly; rare positives
Credit default predictionKS stat, AUC, GiniRanking quality; single threshold needed

Medical diagnosisRecall, SpecificityMissing disease is catastrophic
Search rankingNDCG, MAP, MRROrder matters; top results most important
Revenue forecastingMAPE, RMSEInterpretable, same scale as revenue
Balanced classificationAUC-ROC, F1Balanced classes, need overall quality
🎯 Interview Gold

"I always ask: what is the business cost of false positives vs false negatives? For credit risk, a false negative (missing a default) is far worse — I optimize recall. For spam filtering, false positives (blocking real email) destroy trust — I optimize precision. Accuracy is a trap metric for imbalanced datasets. I use PR-AUC for imbalanced, AUC-ROC for balanced, and always validate the chosen metric with the business stakeholder."
