Probability Theory
The mathematical language of uncertainty — every ML model is fundamentally making probabilistic statements, whether it admits it or not.
Basic Rules
$$\begin{aligned} \mathbb{P}(A \cup B) = \mathbb{P}(A) + \mathbb{P}(B) - \mathbb{P}(A \cap B) \\ \mathbb{P}(A \cap B) = \mathbb{P}(A) \cdot \mathbb{P}(B \mid A) \\ \mathbb{P}(A \mid B) = \mathbb{P}(A \cap B) / \mathbb{P}(B) \\ \text{Independent: } \mathbb{P}(A \cap B) = \mathbb{P}(A) \cdot \mathbb{P}(B) \end{aligned}$$
Bayes' Theorem
$$\begin{aligned} P(H \mid E) = \frac{P(E \mid H) \cdot P(H)}{P(E)} \\[6pt] \text{Posterior} = \frac{\text{Likelihood} \times \text{Prior}}{\text{Evidence}} \end{aligned}$$
📖 Rare Disease Trap

A test is 99% accurate for a disease affecting 1% of people. You test positive. P(sick|positive) = 0.99×0.01/(0.99×0.01+0.01×0.99) ≈ 50%! The low prior (1%) kills the intuition. Bayes corrects your panic.

Expectation & Variance
$$\begin{aligned} E[X] = \sum x_i P(x_i) \quad \text{(discrete)} \\ \mathrm{Var}(X) = E[X^2] - (E[X])^2 \\ \mathrm{Var}(aX+b) = a^2 \mathrm{Var}(X) \\ \mathrm{Cov}(X,Y) = E\!\left[(X - \mu_x)(Y - \mu_y)\right] \\ \rho(X,Y) = \mathrm{Cov}(X,Y) / (\sigma_x \sigma_y) \quad \text{(Pearson r)} \end{aligned}$$
MLE vs MAP
  • MLE: θ̂ = argmax P(data|θ). No prior. Logistic regression minimizes CE = maximizes Bernoulli log-likelihood.
  • MAP: θ̂ = argmax P(θ|data). Includes prior.
💡 Key insight

Ridge regression (L2) = MAP with Gaussian prior. Lasso (L1) = MAP with Laplace prior. Math and ML are the same thing.

🎯 Interview Gold

"Cross-entropy loss comes from maximizing log-likelihood under a Bernoulli/Categorical distribution.
